# Day 12 — Making Plans (Near-Future Intent)

### Chinese (Simplified)

- Sentence: 我明天要去市场。
- Pinyin: wǒ míngtiān yào qù shìchǎng
- Grammar: **要 + verb** signals an upcoming, intended action (“going to do …”).
- Translation: I will go to the market tomorrow.

### Russian

- Sentence: Завтра я пойду на рынок.
- IPA: [ˈzaftrə ja pɐjˈdu na ˈrɨnək]
- Grammar: **Пойду** is the perfective future of *идти* (“will go once”); **завтра** = tomorrow.
- Translation: I will go to the market tomorrow.

### Spanish

- Sentence: Mañana iré al mercado.
- IPA: [maˈɲana iˈɾe al meɾˈkaðo]
- Grammar: Simple-future **iré** (yo form of *ir*); **mañana** precedes or follows the verb phrase.
- Translation: I will go to the market tomorrow.

### French

- Sentence: Demain, j’irai au marché.
- IPA: [dəˈmɛ̃ ʒiˈʁe o maʁˈʃe]
- Grammar: **Irai** is simple-future of *aller*; comma after **Demain** is optional but common for emphasis.
- Translation: I will go to the market tomorrow.

### Arabic (MSA)

- Sentence: سأذهب إلى السوق غدًا.
- IPA: [saˈʔaðhab ʔilaː sːuːq ˈɣadan]
- Grammar: Prefix **سـ** on present verb forms a near-future; **غدًا** = tomorrow.
- Translation: I will go to the market tomorrow.

### Japanese

- Sentence: 明日市場へ行きます。
- Romaji: ashita ichiba e ikimasu
- Grammar: Non-past polite **行きます** + time adverb **明日** expresses future intent.
- Translation: I will go to the market tomorrow.
