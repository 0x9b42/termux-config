# Day 13 — Expressing Ability (“Can / Be Able To”)

### Chinese (Simplified)

- Sentence: 我可以说一点中文。
- Pinyin: wǒ kěyǐ shuō yìdiǎn zhōngwén
- Grammar: **可以 + verb** = “can / may”; **一点** “a little”.
- Translation: I can speak a little Chinese.

### Russian

- Sentence: Я могу немного говорить по-китайски.
- IPA: [ja mɐˈɡu nʲɪˈmnoɡə ɡɐvɐˈrʲitʲ pɐ kʲɪˈtajskʲɪ]
- Grammar: **Могу** is 1-sg of *мочь* (“can”); adverb **немного** “a little”.
- Translation: I can speak a little Chinese.

### Spanish

- Sentence: Puedo hablar un poco de chino.
- IPA: [ˈpweðo aˈβlaɾ um ˈpoko ðe ˈtʃino]
- Grammar: **Poder** in 1-sg **puedo**; **un poco de** “a bit of”.
- Translation: I can speak a little Chinese.

### French

- Sentence: Je peux parler un peu chinois.
- IPA: [ʒə pø paʁle œ̃ pø ʃinwa]
- Grammar: **Peux** is 1-sg of *pouvoir*; **un peu** “a little”.
- Translation: I can speak a little Chinese.

### Arabic (MSA)

- Sentence: أستطيع أن أتحدث قليلاً من الصينية.
- IPA: [ʔas.tˤaˈtiːʕ ʔan ʔa.taˈħadʰa qaˈliːlan min asːiːˈnijja]
- Grammar: Verb **أستطيع** (“I can”) + **أن** + present verb; **قليلاً** “a little”.
- Translation: I can speak a little Chinese.

### Japanese

- Sentence: 私は中国語を少し話せます。
- Romaji: watashi wa chūgokugo o sukoshi hanasemasu
- Grammar: Verb potential form **話せます** (“can speak”); **少し** “a little”.
- Translation: I can speak a little Chinese.
