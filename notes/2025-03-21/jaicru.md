1. Je vois les regrets qui frappent à la porte, j’essaie de rester forte

(I see the regrets that knock on the door, I try to stay strong)

Je vois → "I see".

Je = "I" (subject pronoun)

vois = "see" (1st person singular of voir – to see, present tense)


les regrets → "the regrets".

les = plural definite article ("the")

regrets = noun, plural of regret (same meaning in English)


qui frappent à la porte → "that knock on the door".

qui = relative pronoun (who/that, referring to regrets)

frappent = "knock" (3rd person plural of frapper – to knock, present tense)

à la porte = "at the door" (à = at, la porte = the door)


j’essaie de rester forte → "I try to stay strong".

j’essaie = "I try" (1st person singular of essayer – to try, present tense)

de rester = "to stay" (rester = to stay, and de is required before an infinitive verb)

forte = "strong" (feminine form of fort, because je refers to a female speaker)

2. Je vois mes pensées, les « comment » les « pourquoi », tout me ramène à toi

(I see my thoughts, the 'hows' and the 'whys', everything brings me back to you)

Je vois mes pensées → "I see my thoughts".

mes = "my" (plural possessive adjective, because pensées is plural)

pensées = "thoughts" (feminine plural noun, from penser – to think)


les « comment » les « pourquoi » → "the 'hows' the 'whys'"

comment = "how" and pourquoi = "why"

They are treated as nouns here, meaning questions


tout me ramène à toi → "everything brings me back to you".

tout = "everything" (singular pronoun)

me ramène = "brings me back" (3rd person singular of ramener – to bring back, with me as the object pronoun)

à toi = "to you" (à + stressed pronoun toi)

3. J’ai vu tes sourires devenir perle rare, nos projets perdre espoir

(I saw your smiles become a rare gem, our projects lose hope)

J’ai vu → "I saw".

J’ = je (I)

ai vu = passé composé of voir – "to see" (ai is the auxiliary, vu is the past participle)


tes sourires → "your smiles".

tes = "your" (possessive adjective, plural because sourires is plural)

sourires = "smiles" (plural noun, masculine)


devenir perle rare → "become a rare gem".

devenir = "to become" (infinitive verb)

perle rare = "rare pearl" (feminine noun perle with adjective rare)


nos projets perdre espoir → "our projects lose hope".

nos = "our" (plural possessive adjective)

projets = "projects" (plural noun)

perdre espoir = "lose hope" (perdre = "to lose", espoir = "hope", masculine noun)

4. J’ai vu tout s’enfuir, c’est qu’on n’y croyait plus, pourtant j’ai cru, oui j’ai cru

(I saw everything slip away, we didn’t believe in it anymore, yet I believed, yes I believed)

J’ai vu tout s’enfuir → "I saw everything slip away".

s’enfuir = "to run away / slip away" (reflexive verb in the infinitive)


c’est qu’on n’y croyait plus → "it’s that we didn’t believe in it anymore".

c’est que = "it’s that" (phrase introducing a reason/explanation)

on croyait = "we believed" (imperfect of croire)

n’y... plus = "no longer in it" (n’... plus = negation, y = refers to something previously mentioned)


pourtant j’ai cru, oui j’ai cru → "yet I believed, yes I believed".

pourtant = "yet" / "however"

j’ai cru = passé composé of croire (to believe)

5. J’ai cru qu’on n’serait jamais comme les autres

(I believed that we would never be like the others)

qu’on n’serait jamais → "that we would never be".

qu’ = "that" (conjunction introducing a subordinate clause)

on serait = conditional tense of être ("we would be")

n’... jamais = negation (never)
