# Day 14 — Expressing Desire (“Want to…”)

### Chinese (Simplified)

- Sentence: 我想吃苹果。
- Pinyin: wǒ xiǎng chī píngguǒ  
- Grammar: **想 + verb** = “want to do (something)”
- Translation: I want to eat an apple.

---

### Russian

- Sentence: Я хочу съесть яблоко.  
- IPA: [ja xɐˈt͡ɕu ˈsjestʲ ˈjæbləkə]  
- Grammar: **Хочу** is 1-sg of *хотеть* (“to want”); **съесть** = perfective “to eat”
- Translation: I want to eat an apple.

---

### Spanish

- Sentence: Quiero comer una manzana.  
- IPA: [ˈkjeɾo koˈmeɾ ˈuna manˈθana] (Spain) / [manˈsana] (Latin Am.)  
- Grammar: **Quiero** is 1-sg of *querer*; **comer** = infinitive “to eat”
- Translation: I want to eat an apple.

---

### French

- Sentence: Je veux manger une pomme.  
- IPA: [ʒə vø mɑ̃ʒe yn pɔm]  
- Grammar: **Veux** is 1-sg of *vouloir*; **manger** = “to eat”
- Translation: I want to eat an apple.

---

### Arabic (MSA)

- Sentence: أريد أن آكل تفاحة.  
- IPA: [ʔuˈriːdu ʔan ˈʔaːkul tufˈfaːħa]  
- Grammar: **أريد أن + verb** = “I want to...”; verb **آكل** = “I eat”
- Translation: I want to eat an apple.

---

### Japanese

- Sentence: 私はりんごを食べたいです。  
- Romaji: watashi wa ringo o tabetai desu  
- Grammar: **verb stem + たい** = “want to (do something)”
- Translation: I want to eat an apple.
